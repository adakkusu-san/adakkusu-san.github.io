The Fairly Odd Parents

1999?

(ey this is the first review i did for now)

So, yeah, this is a cartoon about Timmy and his OddParents that was good at first seasons. I always watched it, it was fun, but... as it dragged on the seasons, it got worse.
First off all, Poof is a stupid character. I don't know why they had to introduce him there, and I don't know either why they just had to keep him for so long. He was mostly a filler.
Now, about the dog [todo write name] Sparky. Honestly, I liked him. I liked him more than the baby and the next character. He was good, but where did he go?
The next character being some stupid girl [todo write name] named Chloe who managed to share Timmy's OddParents with her. I mean... why tho. The shortage is clearly a plot thing. Also, why exactly Timmy's ones? Couldn't they go for any other ones? Couldn't they just 'create' some new ones? Couldn't-- eh. What they did could be a way to make matters worse.
And they really did. The fact that the annoying girl is treated in a better way than Timmy (whose fairies belong to), makes me wonder... why don't his OddParents take care more of him? Why is the annoying girl just annoying for no reason? Also, the cover in which Timmy's OddParents put more attention to the annoying one makes me sick. (later edit: It's like he's being betrayed by his own fairies)
Honestly, the dog was a better character. They should have just kept the dog and have said the baby bye, but nooooo. They decided to make both dissapear and introduce an annoying character. The show couldn't get any worse from there.
Also, in more later seasons, Timmy's fairies don't mind being seen around, and I think that's against what they were going for...
Not to mention that in recent seasons, the quality seems to have gotten a bit worse, so... the show's ending (un)fortunately. I don't know what to say... It's gonna end having that annoying character still in.
Also in recent seasons, Timmy's fairies having the power to teleport out of danger, yet not using it at the right time. They also began losing their wands for the plot, even tho in an episode I remember, Da Rules forbid it.

It was fairly good in the beginning.

And, yes, I said I like the dog. Yes, that fairy dog. fite me
